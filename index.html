About

Long before I called myself a designer, I was the kid who couldn't leave a blank page alone. Notebook margins turned into logo sketches. While other kids played outside, I was busy inventing imaginary brands and drawing characters that didn't exist anywhere but my head.

"No matter what the assignment is, they always find a way to turn it into an art project."— a teacher's note I never forgot

That habit never really left — it just grew up. I studied design principles, worked inside branding projects that shape how a company is seen and understood, and learned that good design isn't the version that looks finished. It's the one that communicates, engages and leaves an impression.

Today my craft sits across packaging, brand identity, social content and UI/UX — built on top of a technical foundation in IT infrastructure and network security that most designers never touch. Different tools, same instinct: take what something stands for, and give it a shape people remember.

Visual Storytelling

Every design choice — color, type, layout — is made to say something specific about the brand behind it.

Systems, Not One-Offs

Logos, packaging and digital content built to work together as one coherent identity, not isolated assets.

Detail-Obsessed Craft

A technical background means production-ready files, clean handoffs and no surprises at print or launch.
